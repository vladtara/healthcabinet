# HealthCabinet Pitch Deck

Audience: investors, pilot partners, design collaborators, and technical stakeholders who need a concise product story.

Core message: HealthCabinet turns fragmented lab documents into a longitudinal health intelligence product that people can actually understand and trust.

## Slide 1 — Cover

**Headline:** HealthCabinet  
**Subheadline:** Your health data, finally understood.

- AI-powered interpretation for lab reports and health documents
- Built for people who test repeatedly and need context, not just files
- Desktop-first, clinical-workstation UX instead of generic wellness UI

**Speaker note:** Lead with the positioning sentence. Labs give people numbers; HealthCabinet gives them memory, interpretation, and trend intelligence.

## Slide 2 — The Problem

**Headline:** Health data exists, but meaning is missing.

- Lab results arrive as PDFs, screenshots, or paper printouts
- Each result is a snapshot, with no patient-owned longitudinal memory
- Patients google abnormal numbers, panic, and still do not understand the trend
- Chronic-condition users repeat this loop every few months

**Speaker note:** The problem is not data generation. The problem is interpretation, continuity, and ownership.

## Slide 3 — The Target User

**Headline:** Start with repeated testers.

- Chronic condition managers who test every quarter
- Health-conscious self-testers who buy private lab panels
- A solo-founder-friendly B2C wedge with clear repeat usage

**Speaker note:** Emphasize repeat behavior. The best user is not a one-time uploader. The best user is someone whose data compounds over time.

## Slide 4 — The Product

**Headline:** Upload once. Understand immediately. Learn over time.

- Upload a PDF or photo from any lab
- Extract values into a structured biomarker table
- Show status, ranges, and trends in a dense clinical UI
- Generate plain-language AI interpretation grounded in the uploaded record

**Speaker note:** This slide is the moment where the product becomes concrete. The table-first UI matters because it signals seriousness and supports dense medical data.

## Slide 5 — The Core Loop

**Headline:** Import → understand → trust the platform more.

1. User uploads a new document.
2. The system reads, extracts, normalizes, and stores values.
3. The dashboard updates the user’s history and trend signal.
4. AI explains the latest picture in plain language.

**Speaker note:** Each upload increases product value. That compounding loop is the heart of retention.

## Slide 6 — Why It Is Different

**Headline:** HealthCabinet wins on memory, context, and trust.

- Universal document intelligence instead of narrow lab integrations
- Context-first onboarding that creates value before the first upload
- Cross-upload interpretation instead of one-document summaries
- Clinical workstation visual language that feels credible, not playful

**Speaker note:** Avoid saying “AI app.” Say “personal health intelligence platform.” The differentiation is the combination, not a single feature.

## Slide 7 — Trust And Safety

**Headline:** Sensitive product, serious handling.

- EU data residency target: AWS `eu-central-1`
- GDPR-aware consent, export, and deletion flows
- AES-256 encryption posture and protected token handling
- AI framed as informational, not diagnostic
- Admin correction workflow for extraction edge cases

**Speaker note:** Health products die on trust failure. This is not a compliance footnote; it is part of the product.

## Slide 8 — What Is Already In The Repo

**Headline:** This is not a deck-only idea.

- Authentication and consent-aware registration
- Medical onboarding and profile capture
- Document upload, status streaming, and partial extraction handling
- Biomarker dashboard with trends and AI clinical notes
- Admin operations surface for metrics and review workflows

**Speaker note:** This is the credibility slide. Make it clear there is already product surface, not just strategy language.

## Slide 9 — Business Model

**Headline:** Freemium first, premium intelligence later.

- Free tier proves the upload-to-insight loop
- Paid tier can unlock deeper AI memory, richer trend history, and premium workflows
- Future expansion can include storage upgrades, family plans, and doctor-share features

**Speaker note:** Keep monetization disciplined. The immediate goal is validating the core loop, not overbuilding billing.

## Slide 10 — Go-To-Market

**Headline:** Start where repeated testing already exists.

- Chronic condition communities
- Private lab and self-testing audiences
- Founder-led demos and product storytelling
- Clinical-workstation aesthetic as a memorability advantage

**Speaker note:** The best early GTM channels are trust-heavy communities where people already manage recurring lab work.

## Slide 11 — Roadmap

**Headline:** Prove the loop, then deepen the intelligence.

- Nail upload accuracy and time-to-first-insight
- Increase repeat uploads and multi-document histories
- Expand the AI companion and doctor-share workflows
- Move from Ukraine MVP footing into broader EU expansion

**Speaker note:** The roadmap should feel like compounding depth, not random features.

## Slide 12 — Ask

**Headline:** Help validate the product with real users and real documents.

- Early design and product feedback
- Pilot users with recurring lab workflows
- Strategic partners who care about trustworthy health data UX

**Speaker note:** End with a concrete ask. Do not end on features; end on momentum and collaboration.

## Design Direction For The Deck

- Use the same Arctic Blue clinical-workstation language as the product UX spec.
- Prefer dense layouts, framed panels, and table previews over generic startup blobs.
- Reserve bright color for navigation emphasis and health status semantics.
- When screenshots are available, replace mock tables with the real dashboard and document cabinet.
